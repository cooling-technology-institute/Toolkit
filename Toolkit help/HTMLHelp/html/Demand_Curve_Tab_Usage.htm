Demand Curve Tab Usage

 

 

Entering the values of wet bulb temperature, range, elevation and clicking on ‘Calculate’ brings up the appropriate group of Demand Curves. These curves represent the solution of the Merkel equation over a wide range of liquid-to-gas ratio’s (L/G’s), and are so called since they represent the demand imposed on the cooling tower by the environmental & process variables and each one represents a specific approach temperature (see glossary).

 

Entering a value of L/G and clicking on ‘Calculate’ will display a vertical line at the selected L/G value and intersect the characteristic curve. This intersection defines the approach temperature at the selected L/G ratio. The numerical value of the approach temperature and the KaV/L are displayed in the output box.

Entering a value of of the User Approach ‘Target Approach’ and clicking on ‘Calculate’ will draw the intersecting approach curve to a known L/G & KaV/L intersection.

 

 

The section of the worksheet labeled "Tower or Fill Characteristic" allows the entering of the fill or tower coefficients ‘C’ & ‘Slope’ as mentioned above. Entering a value of "C" and "slope" and clicking ‘Calculate’ will display the fill characteristic curve from the selected minimum and maximum.

 

Entering a value of L/G and clicking on ‘Calculate’ will display a vertical line at the selected L/G value and intersect the characteristic curve. This intersection defines the approach temperature at the selected L/G ratio. The numerical value of the approach temperature and the KaV/L are displayed in the output box.

 

 

Within the black plot area, the mouse can provide the following useful actions:

 

  • Holding down the left mouse button and dragging down and to the right creates an enlargement box, which when released will zoom to fill the plotting area. Clicking on ‘Calculate’ will return the plot to normal.

  • Positioning the mouse pointer over any curve intersection (or any location in the plot area) will return the KaV/L, L/G and approach value for that location.

  • Holding down the right mouse button and dragging will pan the curves left or right, up or down.

  • Click on the Approach checkbox on the right to hide or show a demand curve from the plot area.

 

With all calculation tabs, the data can be saved to a data file (*.dc) and retrieved from the stored data files. To save data, click on the menubar File, Save, to save to the file indicated at the top of the tab. Or on the menubar, File, Save As, to select the fila name of the data file.
